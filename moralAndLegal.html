MORAL AND LEGAL REASONING

Moral Reasoning

"For prisoners on death row, the decades of harsh conditions are endured in a state of both certainty and uncertainty – the certainty and mounting tension that execution is an inevitability that looms ever closer" (Reprieve 7). This shows the morality of the death punishment; it shows how torturous the wait can be. The everlasting pain it brings to those on row proves the death sentence immoral.

Legal Reasoning

A legal reason as to why the death sentence is immoral and the methods are ridiculous is the 8th amendment: Protection against cruel and unusual punishment. The cruelness of the wait to death is enough to prove that the death sentence is illegal, as it is cruel to make prisoners wait for their inevitable death.
