HISTORICAL CONTEXT

Clutters Case

In 1959, the Clutters case was in the papers -- a case about the murder of 2 children and 2 adults in their own home in a small town called Holcomb, Kansas. Journalist Truman Capote interviewed the killers, Richard "Dick" Hickock and Perry Smith. Eventually, Capote released his research as a book titled, "In Cold Blood". The case was so depressing and gruesome that Capote killed himself by overdose.

Death Penalty during the 1950s

While this case was occuring, the United States was utilizing the death penalty for crimes such as treason and murder. An example of treason was the case of the Rosenbergs -- a family killed by the government because of the fear of communism in 1953. This case highlights the morals and legality of the death penalty; In short, did the Rosenbergs deserve to die over treason?

Death by Hanging
