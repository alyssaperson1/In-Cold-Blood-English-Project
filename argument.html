ARGUMENT

Claim

Although the death penalty was socially accepted in the 1950s, the methods and consequences were absurd: therefore, a life-sentence in prison is a far more reasonable punishment.

Evidence

  • "'You can wait around for the rope, but not me.' And from that moment, he refused to touch food or water or even say a word to anybody... over the next nine weeks, his weight fell from 165 to 115 pounds," (Capote 318). This piece of evidence describes how Perrys mental state deteriorated through the five years wait on death row. Rather than wait for death to come he’d rather starve himself to death. This can parallel to how long some prisoners wait on death row and how they feel in that state, proving the death sentence to be inhumane; therefore, a life sentence would be far better.
  • "'They don't feel nothing. Drop, snap, and that's it.' 'Are you sure? I was standing right close. I could hear him gasping for breath.' 'Uh-huh, but he didn't feel nothing. Wouldn't be humane if he did.' 'Well. And I suppose they feed them a lot of pills. Sedatives.' 'Hell, no. Against the rules.'" (Capote 340) When observing the evidence of the guard and interviewer given throughout this scene, it's clear how little the prisoners pain is taken when killing a person on death row. This helps show how little prisoners wellbeing are taken when there finally being killed, proving that the methods of death sentences are insane.
  • "It has been proven many times that the death penalty is substantially more expensive than judicial systems that do not use it....The cost of each death penalty trial and execution typically ranges from $1.5 to 3 million while the cost of imprisoning someone for life ranges from $600000 to $1.1 million" (Spangenberg & Walsh 7) This proves that not only are the methods used to kill people are outrageous, but also that the prices for these methods are insane. By using a life sentence instead of a death sentence, the prices of the methods for the death sentences will not be used, but rather the life sentence price.

Counter Argument

Some people argue that the death penalty is a fair punishment. They make a fair point that an eye is for an eye. However, evidence from the JFK assassination demonstrates how fighting violence with more violence does not equal a peaceful outcome. More likely just creating a never-ending cycle of said violence. Therefore, a more peaceful approach would be to let them serve a sentence that a hanging.
